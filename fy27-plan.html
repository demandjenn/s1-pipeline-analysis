S1 Pipeline Plan — FY2027

Team Source Distribution & Efficiency Analysis

Executive Summary

This plan outlines how marketing and sales development will deliver 1,789 S1 opportunities in FY27, supporting the company's $12.0M ARR target. The plan operates within a $4.0M marketing budget by leveraging efficiency improvements across all sourcing teams.

The most significant structural change is a deliberate rebalancing of team source distribution. Marketing Sourced remains the primary engine at 53% of all S1s (956), but AE Outbound expands substantially from 19% to 30% (538 S1s). BDR Outbound holds steady at 12% with a sharper focus on ENT and MM segments, while Partner Sourced scales modestly to 4%. The rebalancing reduces single-point-of-failure risk on marketing while capitalizing on the strong AE outbound trajectory seen in FY26.

The critical enabler is efficiency improvement. The plan assumes front-loaded efficiency ramps (33% marketing improvement in Q1, 100% AE outbound ramp in Q1) with gains moderating through the year. These assumptions are aggressive but grounded in the AE productivity trends observed in Q3-Q4 FY26, where S1 per AE jumped from 3 to 8. Segment-level conversion rates (ENT at 6%, MM at 10.6%, SMB at 19.5%) are based on trailing averages and translate the S1 volume into $4.4M in marketing-sourced ARR, representing roughly 37% of the total ARR target.

Key Insights

  • Volume Growth: The FY27 plan targets 1,789 total S1s (up from ~747 actual in the trailing 4 quarters), driven by scaling efficiency across all teams.
  • Marketing Sourced Dominance: Marketing Sourced grows to encompass 956 S1s (53% of total), reflecting continued reliance on marketing as the primary demand engine.
  • AE Outbound Expansion: AE Outbound ramps aggressively to 538 S1s (30% of total), up from 19% historically — this is the single largest shift in team source distribution.
  • BDR Refocus: BDR Outbound holds at 223 S1s (12%), down from 26% historically. This reflects both a deliberate segment shift — BDRs were moved off SMB to focus exclusively on ENT and MM — as well as headcount changes and evolving performance trends in FY26.
  • Partner Growth: Partner Sourced scales to 70 S1s (4%), up from ~2% historically — this relies on 75% quarterly growth assumptions across segments.

Key Assumptions

  • Marketing Efficiency: Decreasing cost per opportunity with 33% improvement in Q1, 17% in Q2, then stabilizes and moderates through year-end.
  • AE Outbound Ramp: Aggressive ramp with 100% efficiency in Q1 (doubling from current), 40% in Q2, 30% in Q3, 35% in Q4 — reflecting significant investment in this channel.
  • BDR Outbound Gains: Moderate efficiency improvements at 53% in Q1, 13% in Q2, 20% in Q3, 7% in Q4 — aligns with declining productivity trend.
  • Partner Efficiency: Assumes consistent 75% quarterly improvement across all segments — this is an aggressive assumption that warrants ongoing monitoring.
  • Front-Loaded Risk: Key risk is that efficiency gains are front-loaded (especially AE), requiring rapid ramp-up in Q1 and successful execution early in the year.

Segment Strategy

  • SMB Dominance: SMB dominates at 1,082 S1s (60% of total), driven by Marketing Sourced (651) and AE Outbound (376), creating pipeline breadth.
  • MM Contribution: MM contributes 397 S1s (22%), with a balanced split between Marketing (204), BDR (96), and AE (81), providing stability.
  • ENT Quality Focus: ENT accounts for 321 S1s (18%), but these carry the highest AOV ($332K vs $102K MM, $14K SMB) — critical for hitting the $12M ARR target.
  • Deliberate Mix: The segment mix reflects a deliberate shift: SMB volume creates pipeline breadth while ENT quality drives ARR concentration and revenue growth.
  • Conversion Basis: Key conversion rates used: ENT 6%, MM 10.6%, SMB 19.5% (S1 to Closed/Won), based on trailing 8-quarter averages.

Budget Strategy & Risk Assessment

  • Front-Loaded Spend: Total modified budget is $4.0M for FY27, with front-loaded spend ($1.0M in Q1, $1.8M in Q2, $600K each in Q3/Q4).
  • Budget Allocation: Distribution is 82% New Business, 10% Retention, 8% Operational, and variable Awareness component across quarters.
  • Efficiency as Enabler: Efficiency improvements across marketing, AE outbound, and BDR teams are the primary lever allowing the plan to deliver on S1 targets within this budget envelope.
  • Key Risks: Front-loaded budget means Q3-Q4 rely heavily on pipeline generated from earlier quarters converting; efficiency assumptions are aggressive and require disciplined execution.
